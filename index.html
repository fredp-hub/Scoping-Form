CUI Scoping Discovery Questionnaire

This questionnaire follows the CMMC Assessment Process (CAP) methodology and is structured around all 14 NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 practice families. Questions are written at the CCP level — each asks not only whether a control exists, but how it is implemented, who owns it, and what evidence exists to demonstrate it.

Answer as specifically as possible. Gaps identified here become your roadmap. Inaccurate or overstated answers will be surfaced during assessment — honesty now saves time and money later.

3.1.1 / 3.1.2Authorized access & least privilegeCritical
How is access to CUI systems provisioned, reviewed, and revoked?
Assessors will examine the provisioning process, review access logs, and interview system administrators. They need to see that access is need-to-know, not need-to-not-deny. Key: who approves access requests and what is the documented process?
Evidence a C3PAO will request
  • Access control policy and procedures
  • Sample access request/approval records
  • Access review logs (last 12 months)
  • Terminated employee list cross-referenced with active accounts
3.1.3Control CUI flowCritical
How do you prevent CUI from flowing to unauthorized systems, users, or external destinations?
This is often a gap. Controls must be technical, not just policy. DLP, email filtering rules, blocked USB ports, and network segmentation all count. Policy-only controls without technical enforcement are not sufficient.

3.5
Identification & Authentication
11 practices — MFA, passwords, and identity management for CUI systems
▾
3.5.3Multi-factor authenticationCritical
Is multi-factor authentication (MFA) enforced for all access to CUI systems — including local, remote, and privileged access?
MFA is non-negotiable for CMMC L2. MFA must be enforced via Conditional Access or equivalent — not just available. Assessors will test this by attempting to authenticate without MFA. "We tell people to use it" is not sufficient.
Evidence a C3PAO will request
  • Conditional Access policy screenshots showing MFA enforcement
  • MFA enrollment report (all users)
  • List of accounts excluded from MFA with documented rationale
  • Live demonstration: assessor will attempt login without MFA
3.5.7 / 3.5.8Password complexity & prohibitionHigh
Describe your password policy for CUI system accounts.
NIST SP 800-63B guidance (which CMMC aligns to) emphasizes length over complexity. Minimum 12 characters is standard. Assessors will verify the policy is technically enforced, not just documented.

3.3
Audit & Accountability
9 practices — Logging, monitoring, and audit trail for CUI systems
▾
3.3.1 / 3.3.2Audit log creation & protectionCritical
What events are logged on CUI systems, and where are logs stored?
Logs must cover logins, failed logins, privilege escalation, account management, and object access on CUI systems. Logs stored only on the source system are insufficient — they must be forwarded to a protected centralized location that cannot be modified by the system being logged.
Evidence a C3PAO will request
  • SIEM dashboard screenshots showing log ingestion from CUI systems
  • Audit log policy document specifying what is logged and retention period
  • Sample audit log showing required event types
  • Evidence logs are protected from modification (access controls on log storage)

3.13
System & Communications Protection
16 practices — Network architecture, encryption, and boundary protection
▾
3.13.1 / 3.13.2Boundary protection & network monitoringCritical
Describe your network architecture — specifically how the CUI environment is separated from external networks and internal non-CUI systems.
Boundary protection requires more than a firewall at the perimeter. The CUI environment needs its own defined boundary with controlled ingress/egress. Flat networks where CUI lives alongside general corporate traffic are a critical finding.
Evidence a C3PAO will request
  • Network topology / architecture diagram
  • Firewall configuration export or screenshot of ruleset
  • VLAN configuration showing CUI segment isolation
  • Penetration test or vulnerability scan results (last 12 months)
3.13.8 / 3.13.10Encryption in transit & at restCritical
Is CUI encrypted in transit and at rest? Describe the specific encryption mechanisms used.
CMMC requires FIPS 140-2 validated cryptographic modules. Not all commercial encryption meets this bar. TLS 1.2 minimum required; TLS 1.0/1.1 are findings. BitLocker without TPM protection has weaknesses assessors look for.

3.14
System & Information Integrity
7 practices — Malware protection, patching, and security alerts
▾
3.14.1 / 3.14.2Malicious code protectionCritical
What endpoint protection solution is deployed on CUI systems? Describe detection, response, and update mechanisms.
AV alone is generally insufficient. Assessors look for EDR capability with behavioral detection, automatic updates, and managed response. Personal/consumer AV products (Windows Defender standalone, Malwarebytes free) are commonly flagged.
3.14.4 / 3.14.5Patch managementHigh
Describe your patch management process for CUI systems — how quickly are critical patches applied and how is compliance tracked?
CMMC requires timely patching. Industry standard is Critical patches within 30 days, High within 60 days. Assessors will run vulnerability scans and check for known CVEs on CUI systems. Unpatched critical vulnerabilities are automatic findings.

3.6.1 / 3.6.2IR capability & reportingCritical
Does your organization have a documented, tested Incident Response Plan (IRP) that covers CUI incidents?
The IRP must include roles and responsibilities, detection/reporting procedures, containment/eradication steps, and recovery procedures. DoD contractors must report cybersecurity incidents affecting CUI to DIBNet within 72 hours. Assessors will ask you to walk through a tabletop scenario.

3.7.1 / 3.7.2 / 3.7.5Controlled maintenance & remote maintenanceMedium
How is maintenance of CUI systems performed — including by third-party vendors or your MSP? What controls ensure CUI is not exposed during maintenance?
Remote maintenance using tools like ConnectWise, Datto RMM, or Kaseya must be authenticated, encrypted, and logged. Maintenance sessions must be terminated when not in use. Vendors cannot maintain a persistent always-on connection to CUI systems without strong controls.

Backup strategy, retention, and recovery testing for CUI systems

Describe your backup and recovery posture for CUI systems.
Backups of CUI data are themselves CUI and must be protected equivalently. Backups stored on the same system or network segment as the primary data provide no real protection against ransomware. Assessors will ask to see recovery test records.
